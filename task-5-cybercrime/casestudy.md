# Part A -- Case Study

## UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime
where attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive
banking details. This usually happens through fake calls, messages, or
links.

A typical scenario begins when a scammer contacts a victim pretending to
be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. They
create urgency, such as claiming there is an issue with the user's
account or a pending payment. The victim is then asked to click a
malicious link or approve a payment request on a UPI app. In some cases,
fraudsters send a "collect request" and mislead the victim into
approving it, thinking they will receive money instead of sending it.

Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use
digital payments and may not be fully aware of such scams. The
consequences can include financial loss, theft of personal data, and
emotional stress.

Such incidents highlight the importance of awareness and caution while
using digital payment platforms. Understanding how these scams work can
help individuals avoid becoming victims and protect their financial
information.
